# Peptides: what works, what's hype, what's dangerous — and how not to get poisoned on the gray market

*A research overview (2026). This is not medical advice — it's a map of the territory: what's approved, what's actually proven, what's myth, and how the market that ships all of this actually works. After reading, everyone makes their own decisions with their own doctor.*

---

## The key points up front (if you only read this)

1. **The gray peptide market is ~90% hype built on ~10% real medicine.** The real medicine is mostly GLP-1 (Ozempic and its relatives) and a handful of approved drugs. Everything else is sold as "research chemical, not for human use" — that is a **legal loophole, not a quality mark**.
2. **A peptide is a tool for a specific problem**, not a "stack to become better." If there's a problem (obesity, a non-healing injury, low libido, visceral fat) → there's a peptide for it. No problem → nearly everything falls into the "not needed" column.
3. **The main risk is not the molecule — it's the PRODUCT.** The exotic fears (cancer from a peptide) are almost all theoretical and based on rodent studies. But endotoxin fever, abscesses from non-sterile injections, and heavy metals in vials are measurable, human, and documented. On the gray market, the dirt in the vial will get you before the peptide itself does.

## 1. What peptides actually are

A peptide is a short chain of amino acids (2–50 of them) — something between an "amino acid" and a "protein." Biologically these are **signaling molecules**: they mimic hormones or growth factors and trigger a specific receptor in the body.

- **Injectable** (the majority) — peptide bonds break down in the stomach, so they're injected subcutaneously (SC) or intramuscularly (IM).
- **Oral** (rare exceptions) — resistant to digestion: MK-677 (not actually a peptide), KPV (absorbed via the PepT1 transporter), BPC-157 (gastro-stable locally in the gut).

Why there's a gray market around them: ~90% of peptides interesting to biohackers **are not approved by any regulator** for any recognized medical condition. They're sold under the label "research / not for human use." Almost all "research-grade" batches are synthesized at ~3–4 factories in China, and Western "brands" simply repackage the same powder.

## 2. What the FDA has actually approved, and what it hasn't (as of 2026)

### Real drugs (human clinical trials exist, approval granted)

| Drug | Class | Approved for |
|---|---|---|
| **Semaglutide** (Ozempic / Wegovy, oral form since Dec-2025) | GLP-1 agonist | type 2 diabetes (2017), obesity (2021) |
| **Tirzepatide** (Mounjaro / Zepbound) | GLP-1 + GIP | diabetes (2022), obesity (2023), sleep apnea (2024) |
| **Liraglutide** | GLP-1 | outdated, weaker than newer options |
| **Orforglipron** | oral GLP-1 (not a peptide) | obesity (Apr-2026), more convenient than injections, but weaker |
| **Tesamorelin** (Egrifta) | GHRH analogue | **the only drug with direct evidence against visceral fat** (in HIV lipodystrophy) |
| **Bremelanotide** (Vyleesi / PT-141) | melanocortin | female hypoactive sexual desire disorder (male — off-label) |
| **Setmelanotide** | MC4R | rare genetic obesity |
| **SS-31 / Elamipretide** | mitochondrial | rare Barth syndrome (notably failed its primary endpoint in the trial) |

### Research / gray — NOT approved (sold as "for research use")
BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, KPV, ipamorelin, CJC-1295, sermorelin, GHRP-2/6, hexarelin, **MK-677**, MOTS-c, humanin, **5-Amino-1MQ**, epitalon, thymalin, thymosin-α1, ARA-290, **melanotan I/II**, PT-141 (gray vials), Semax, Selank, **Dihexa**, and ~200 more compounds.

### The trap of "they were approved again" (important — this is how sellers lie)
- **Late 2023:** The FDA moved ~19 popular peptides (BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, KPV…) to "Category 2" — significant risks (impurities, immunogenicity, no data). Legal compounding pharmacy production in the US came to a halt.
- **February 2026:** an *intention* was announced to return ~14 of the 19 to Category 1. But Melanotan II, GHRP-2/6, and others **remain restricted**.
- **April 2026:** they were formally removed from Category 2 — **because the applications were withdrawn, not because the FDA confirmed safety**.
- **The bottom line:** "removed from Cat. 2" ≠ "legal to compound." That requires a separate procedure that, as of mid-2026, **had not happened**. So any advertisement claiming "FDA approved / legal again" is **a lie**.
- **GLP-1 separately:** legal compounding of cheap Ozempic copies in the US **is dead** (the shortage is over, courts dismissed the challenges, the FDA is moving toward a permanent ban on copies — reason cited: 455+ reports of harm from dosing errors). What's left is either a brand-name prescription or the gray market.

## 3. By class — mechanism, dose, benefits, risks

### 🧠 Brain (nootropics / neuro)
The honest truth: effects on a healthy brain are subtle; the best data is for recovery after injury (stroke, trauma), and much of it comes from Russian clinical research without Western double-blind trials.

- **Semax** (+ N-acetyl variants) — focus, memory, neuroprotection. Raises BDNF/NGF + dopamine, not a stimulant. **Intranasal**, 200–600 mcg/day. Targets: hippocampus, prefrontal cortex. Russian clinical background (approved there for stroke/cognition), almost no Western RCTs. **🟡 The best "clean focus" option with at least some clinical history.**
- **Selank** (+ amidate) — anxiety relief/calm focus without sedation. BDNF + GABA/enkephalins. Intranasal 250–900 mcg/day. **🟡 The Semax+Selank combination is the most sensible entry point into neuropeptides.**
- **Bromantane** (not a peptide) — calm energy + anti-fatigue. Raises dopamine *synthesis* (no rebound/crash). Oral 50–100 mg. **🟢/🟡 Underrated.**
- **Noopept** — memory/focus, BDNF, AMPA modulation. Oral 10–30 mg (with choline, otherwise headaches). Effect is subtle. **🟡 Cheap, oral, low risk.**
- **Cerebrolysin / Cortexin** — neuroregeneration (stroke/TBI/dementia), a mixture of neurotrophins. Injected in courses IM/IV, painful, animal-derived source. **Real trials for pathological conditions**, not for healthy people. **🟡 for recovery · ⚪ to "get smarter".**
- **Dihexa** — extremely potent synaptogenesis (HGF/c-Met pathway agonist). ⚠️ This is a **proliferative, potentially pro-carcinogenic pathway** + zero human data. **🟡/🔴 Risky experiment.**
- **P21 / PE-22-28 / NAP** — neurogenesis/antidepressant. Rodents only (NAP failed its human trial). **⚪ Speculative.**
- **DSIP** — sleep, weak data **🟡**. **Methylene blue** — brain mitochondria, ⚠️ dangerous interaction with antidepressants (serotonin syndrome) **🟡**. **Phenibut** — this is not a nootropic, it's a GABA agonist with **addiction potential and severe withdrawal** **🔴**.

### 💪 Muscle / recovery / growth hormone
- **HGH (somatropin) and secretagogues** (ipamorelin, CJC-1295, sermorelin, GHRP-2/6, hexarelin, MK-677). All ultimately raise growth hormone → IGF-1.
  - **Ipamorelin** — the "cleanest" (no cortisol, prolactin, or hunger spike).
  - **GHRP-6** = intense hunger; **GHRP-2** = cortisol+prolactin up; **hexarelin** = most cortisol + rapid tolerance.
  - **MK-677** (oral) — raises GH/IGF-1, but in a 2-year trial (Nass 2008) **failed everything that mattered**: no strength improvement, no visceral fat reduction, plus increased water retention, elevated glucose and cortisol, and worse insulin sensitivity. Water retention and "puffy face" come from this.
  - **Honest verdict on the class:** secretagogues exist to **restore** growth hormone in deficient and aging individuals. In a healthy young person they push IGF-1 up (see longevity — debatable), and **none of them raise testosterone**.
- **BPC-157** — pro-angiogenesis, ligament/gut repair. Data: rodents for everything "sport-related" + 1 retrospective on 12 patients (no control) + a pilot on 2 people. More sensible taken orally for GI problems. **🟡 for a specific injury or gut issue.**
- **TB-500** — cell migration, healing. Rodents only (human RCT data exists only for corneal healing). **⚪/🟡.**
- **GHK-Cu** — copper peptide. **Topically (cream)** — genuinely works on skin/collagen, controlled trials exist. Injectable/systemic — zero data. **🟡 as a cosmetic.**
- **Follistatin / ACE-031 / myostatin blockers** — theoretically more muscle, but **serious risks** (bleeding with ACE-031 in trials). **🔴 for a recreational user.**

### 🔥 Fat / metabolism
- **GLP-1 (semaglutide, tirzepatide, retatrutide, and newer)** — **this is the category that actually works, powerfully, with a massive evidence base.** If someone asks "do peptides even work?" — here's proof they do. For someone with excess weight, these are life-changing.
  - ⚠️ Key caveat: **25–30% of weight lost is muscle**, not just fat. Without strength training + protein you lose body quality too. Plus GI side effects, and weight rebounds after stopping.
- **Tesamorelin** — the only one with **proven** selective reduction of visceral fat (−15–19%, body weight unchanged). But: data only in HIV patients, ~30% don't respond, the effect **reverses within 3–6 months** after stopping, and IGF-1 rises +81%.
- **SLU-PP-332, 5-Amino-1MQ, MOTS-c** — "exercise mimetics," theoretically burn fat/fix metabolism. **Rodents only, zero human data.** Beautiful mechanisms, safety — completely unknown. **⚪ Speculative experiment, not a bet.**

### ⏳ Longevity / mitochondria
- **NAD+ / NMN / NR** — trendy, "energy and youth." Reality: oral forms are cheap and give the same (un)proven effect as expensive IV drips. **⚪ IV NAD+ drips = mostly expensive placebo theater.**
- **Epitalon, thymalin, Khavinson peptide bioregulators** — Russian gerontology, **all literature from one group, not reproduced in the West**. Hype >> data. **⚪.**
- **Thymosin-α1, SS-31, rapamycin, senolytics (fisetin/quercetin)** — real data exists, but **for sick people** (hepatitis, sepsis, specific diseases), not for healthy people seeking "life extension."

### ❤️ Sex / libido / cosmetics
- **PT-141 (Bremelanotide)** — **the best evidence-backed option for libido.** Works through the brain (not blood vessels like Viagra). Dose 1.75 mg subcutaneous ~45 min before, **episodically**. Side effects: nausea (~40%), blood pressure spike + heart rate decrease for ~12 h (contraindicated with hypertension), with frequent use — possible **lasting pigmentation of the face/gums**. Limit ~8 doses/month. **🟡 for sex, on occasion.**
- **Melanotan II** — for tanning and libido. **🔴 AVOID.** Documented cases include: melanoma, flare-ups of atypical moles, darkening of existing moles, priapism, muscle breakdown + kidney failure, renal infarction, neurological PRES syndrome. Banned in Australia as of Feb-2026 as a drug of abuse (Schedule 9, same as heroin). Especially dangerous for people with high sun exposure.
- **GHK-Cu, Argireline, Matrixyl, and other cosmetic peptides** — topically they gently work on skin/wrinkles, low risk. **🟡 fine as a cream.**

## 4. The Chinese market — how it actually works (most useful if you're buying)

**China produces ~68% of the world's raw peptide material.** Concentrated in: Jiangsu province (~35%, factories GL Biochem, ChinaPeptides), Zhejiang (~25%), Shandong (~20%, cheap/bulk). US imports of peptides from China doubled in 2025 to ~$328M. **Almost every "American" brand is repackaged Chinese raw material.**

### Why impurities are INEVITABLE
Peptides are assembled by SPPS — amino acids are attached one at a time. At each step, some chains come out defective: missed residues, truncated chains, mirror-image isomers. These "fragments" are **structurally nearly identical to the target molecule**, so cheap purification doesn't remove them. That's what's really behind the "99% purity" label.

### Three quality tiers
- **GMP pharma (~25–30%)** — cleanrooms, full QC. Almost impossible to buy directly.
- **Research grade (~40–45%)** — HPLC on most batches, endotoxin testing is occasional. Most of the decent raw material.
- **Cheap tier (~25–35%)** — minimal QC, **template certificates**. Contamination and underdosing live here.

### The Certificate of Analysis (COA) — the most forged document on the market
**~43% of peptides tested by the Janoshik lab in 2024 did not meet their claimed purity.** Typical fraud:
- identical numbers across "different" batches;
- suspiciously round figures (exactly 99.0%);
- reused batch numbers;
- PDFs regenerated with a new seller's name;
- old results passed off as the current product.

### What even an honest COA doesn't cover
- **Endotoxin (LPS)** — bacterial debris that survives sterilization, causes fever/sepsis when injected. A vial can be **sterile and endotoxin-contaminated at the same time**. Requires a separate test (LAL).
- **Sterility** — live bacteria/fungi → abscesses (documented Mycobacterium cases exist).
- **Heavy metals** — invisible to standard tests. Belgian forensic analysis: ~26% of seized injectables had arsenic/lead above safety limits (arsenic up to ×10 over limit).
- **Solvents (TFA and industrial chemicals)** — toxic synthesis residues that purity percentage doesn't capture.
- **Actual mg in the vial** — purity is a **percentage, not a quantity**. A vial can be 99% pure and contain 3 mg instead of the stated 10 (underdosing / "fronting"). In a dataset of 590 BPC-157 samples, dosing variance reached ±85%, with outright molecular substitution occurring.

### How experienced people verify
The standard is the independent lab **Janoshik (Slovakia)**. The discipline: send an **anonymous sample directly**, the seller doesn't see the result in advance, the result goes into a **public database** (the seller can't edit it). The tests are DIFFERENT — order the right ones:
- **HPLC** → purity (%)
- **Mass spec** → is this even the right molecule (catches substitution)
- **Quantification / "ID and amount"** → actual mg (catches underdosing — **the test everyone forgets**)
- **Sterility + Endotoxin (LAL)** → injection safety
Prices: HPLC ~€80–150, full panel ~$660 per vial.

### Storage and reconstitution
Dry powder (lyophilized) — one to two years in the refrigerator. Reconstitute with **bacteriostatic water** (contains benzyl alcohol — inhibits bacteria during repeated needle penetration), drip down the side of the vial, swirl don't shake, store at +2–8 °C for ~28–30 days. **Reconstitution does NOT sterilize already-contaminated powder.**

### Typical scams
Underdosing at full price; wrong molecule; dummy/degraded product; photoshopped certificate; non-existent laboratories; the "we'll reship for free if seized" game (sometimes honest, sometimes a way to take money for a package that was never sent).

### Practical framework for "don't get poisoned and don't get ripped off"
1. Every vial is **guilty until proven innocent** by an anonymous test.
2. For everything: **MS (is it the right thing) + HPLC (purity) + Quantification (how many mg)**.
3. For injectables add **Endotoxin + Sterility**.
4. Anonymous sample → Janoshik → check the public database.
5. Clean aseptic technique on reconstitution.
6. **If you're not experienced with injections — the sterility/technique will harm you before the peptide does.**

## 5. Toxicity and long-term effects — by risk vector

**Counterintuitively: the product is more dangerous than the molecule.**

**Vector 1 — the molecule itself (mostly theoretical):**
- *Immunogenicity* — more often from impurities than from the peptide itself (which is exactly what the FDA cited when placing peptides in Cat. 2).
- *IGF-1 → cancer — MYTH in the form people fear it.* Epidemiology is weak and organ-dependent. The strongest signal is in acromegaly patients (extreme growth hormone for years) — that is **not the relevant model**. The cleanest human analogue is adult growth hormone replacement therapy: cancer **−31% (protective effect)**, not increased. The real, narrow caveat: **do not use if you have active or recent cancer** (IGF-1 may feed an already-existing tumor).
- *GLP-1 → thyroid cancer — MYTH.* The black box warning comes from rodents (human C-cells have few of these receptors). A cohort of 145,000 people: no elevated risk. The caveat applies only to people with hereditary thyroid cancer (MEN2).
- *GLP-1 → muscle loss — REAL.* The most genuine downside.
- *Melanotan II → melanoma* — real human cases documented.

**Vector 2 — the product (THE larger real risk):** online semaglutide labeled "99%" tested at 7.7–14.4% actual content (Venhuis 2024), every vial endotoxin-positive. Seized product found with solvents, arsenic/lead, glass/rubber particles. Non-sterile reconstitution → abscess. **The cancer theories are 5–20 years out and in rodents. Endotoxin fever, abscesses, and heavy metals are this week and measurable.**

**Vector 3 — unknown unknowns:** for BPC-157, TB-500, CJC, ipamorelin, melanotan, MK-677 — **zero long-term human trials.** Even for approved tesamorelin — only 52 weeks of data. **"Safe" here means "no data," not "proven safe."**

## 7. Bottom line: what's actually worth it for whom

Peptides are justified in three situations, not as a "button to become better":
1. **You have a specific deficiency or condition** — obesity → GLP-1; visceral fat → tesamorelin; a non-healing injury → BPC/TB-500; low libido → PT-141; aging skin → GHK-Cu cream.
2. **You're a competitive athlete accepting conscious risk** (growth hormone / IGF) — a professional's game, not a health move.
3. **You're an experimenter doing it for the process** — and then you test every vial.

**What actually works (no fluff):** GLP-1 (for excess weight) · Tesamorelin (visceral fat) · PT-141 (libido) · GHK-Cu cream (skin, ~zero risk) · BPC/TB-500 (a specific injury, if the raw material is clean).

**Hype / not worth the money:** MOTS-c, 5-Amino-1MQ, SLU-PP-332 (zero human data) · epitalon, thymalin, bioregulators · IV NAD+ drips · most "T-boosters" and "longevity stacks."

**Dangerous:** Melanotan I/II (melanoma, especially with sun exposure) · myostatin blockers for recreational users · growth hormone "for a healthy person" (pushes IGF-1 up, doesn't build more muscle than training does, doesn't raise testosterone).

**If you're healthy with no specific problem — you probably don't need peptides.** The basics (sleep, training, protein, not smoking) do more than a shelf full of vials. And if you decide to try — **the main risk is not the molecule, it's the dirt in the vial**: anonymous testing at Janoshik + sterile technique saves you more than any choice of "trendy" peptide.
